LumenFocus Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 June 2026

LumenFocus is a menu-bar app for macOS that reminds you to rest your eyes. We designed it to be private by default. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your data.

The short version

LumenFocus does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. There are no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party SDKs. The developer has no access to your information.

Data stored on your device

To make the app work, the following information is stored locally on your Mac (in macOS UserDefaults and the app sandbox container) and never leaves your device:

  • Your preferences (work / rest durations, smart-pause toggles, ambient sound choice, launch-at-login, menu-bar display options).
  • Eye-care statistics (daily rest counts and screen-time totals, kept for up to about 90 days for the trends and heatmap views).

This data is removed when you delete the app. You can also export your statistics to a CSV file yourself; that file is created only where you choose to save it.

System permissions

  • Notifications (optional): used solely to deliver a local weekly eye-care summary. Nothing is sent over the network.
  • Activity detection: to avoid interrupting you, the app checks on-device signals such as whether a full-screen or video-call app is active, or whether the Mac is idle or locked. These checks happen entirely on your device and are not recorded or transmitted.

Children's privacy

LumenFocus does not collect data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new "last updated" date.
